Jacque Fresco

Futurist and self-described social engineer

Jacque Fresco's Timeline

  • 1916 - born on March 13, grew up in Bensonhurst, in the Brooklyn borough of New York City.
  • 1930 - worked at Douglas Aircraft Company in California. He presented designs including a flying wing and a disk-shaped aircraft.
  • 1942 - was drafted into the United States Army. He was assigned technical design duties for the United States Army Air Forces at Wright Field design laboratories in Dayton, Ohio.
  • Late 1940s - created and was director of Scientific Research Laboratories in Los Angeles. Here he also gave lectures, and taught technical design, meanwhile researching and working on inventions as a freelance inventor and scientific consultant.
  • 1955 - moved to Miami, Florida. He opened a business as a psychological consultant.
  • 1961 - with Pietro Belluschi and C. Frederick Wise, Fresco collaborated on a project known as the Sandwich House. Consisting of mostly prefabricated components, partitions, and aluminum, the project sold houses for $2,950, or $7,500 with foundation and all internal installations.
  • 1969 - Looking Forward was published. Author Ken Keyes Jr., and Jacque Fresco coauthored the book. Looking Forward is a speculative look at the future.
  • 1990 - The Venus Project - Fresco, with Meadows, supported the project in the 1990s through freelance inventing, industrial engineering, conventional architectural modeling, and invention consultations.
  • 2002 - Fresco published his main work The Best That Money Can't Buy.
  • 2006 - William Gazecki directed the semi-biographical film about Fresco, Future by Design.
  • 2008 - Peter Joseph featured Fresco in the film Zeitgeist Addendum where his ideas of the future were given as possible alternatives.
  • 2011 - Zeitgeist: Moving Forward was released in theaters, featuring Fresco.
  • 2012 - Roxanne Meadows released a film, Paradise or Oblivion, summarizing the goals and proposals of the Venus Project.
  • 2016 - Jacque Fresco received a Novus Summit award for City Design/Community. Novus Summit is supported by UN DESA (United Nations Department of Economic and Social Affairs)
  • 2017 - Fresco died on May 18, 2017 in his sleep at his home in Sebring, Florida, from complications of Parkinson's disease at the age of 101.
